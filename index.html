Campus Life

School yard

Carnival and Activities

Students all ages can go play here. During recess they can go on the playstructure or play kickball, basketball, volleyball, wallball, four square (not a ball), race, read outside if you want, and other fun things! Although middle schoolers only have lunch recess we get to rotate areas every week! So we all have a chance to play in a different area. There's also a chicken coop and a big digout area that's filled with woodchucks, logs, bushes, trees, and flowers! Usual at the end of the school year theres a carnival! It takes place in the school yard and fun tents and games are set up. To start, the grades from oldest to youngest all performs a dance!

School Library

Books and Activities

The Lawton school library is opened everyday of the week until Friday. You can always go there to ask the librarian for recommendations on a specific type of book or genre, or learn how to do one of the activities. During recess you can go to the library and start a knitting or weaving project.

School Auditorium

Concerts and plays

Lawton's auditorium, gymnasium, or just gym is a place where plays, basketball, concerts and teacher talent shows takes place! All grade preform in there like kindergardenrs and first graders sing, second grade ukele, thrid grade recorders, fourth orchestra or band, and so on! From kindergarden to second grade they have perception motor where the PTA sets up fun courses for the kids to work on! To bad they don't allow 3rd and up to have it anymore.

What's New

  • Chicken Coop

    Right behind the kickball field there is a chicken coop. Trees and rocks are put there along with a giant cage and a chicken coop build inside, aswell as extra space for chicken feeders and just for the chickens to roam about. Students before school, recess, and after class can go see the chickens. Four chickens── two brown ones, one white, and a mix of both!

  • BACR Afteschool program

    There are multiple afterschool program at Lawton. One for instance is BACR, or Bay Area Community Resources. You go to the basketball court and sign in after school and you have a period of free time. You can eat snacks, play, or have 'supper', which is basically school lunch that haven't been eaten. We then move on to either projects or do homework. If it's projects, we do homework after, vise versa. You can play after that and the program ends at 6, though you can pick up your child anytime, but it's better to pick them up later otherwise the program might not keep you.
